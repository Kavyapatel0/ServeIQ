FRONTEND PHASE 4 – POS (Point of Sale)
Objective

The POS is the heart of the restaurant.

Every dine-in order starts here.

The cashier/waiter spends nearly 90% of their time on this screen.

Therefore the POS must be

Extremely fast
Very responsive
Minimal clicks
Large touch-friendly buttons
Keyboard shortcuts (future)
Mobile/Tablet friendly

The user should be able to create an order within 30–40 seconds.

Purpose

FRONTEND PHASE 5 – Kitchen Dashboard
Objective

The Kitchen Dashboard (Kitchen Display System or KDS) is where chefs manage food preparation.

Its responsibilities are:

Receive new orders from the POS.
Display them in preparation order.
Allow chefs to update order status.
Show live updates without refreshing.
Notify waiters when food is ready.

This replaces the traditional paper ticket system.

Purpose

The Kitchen Dashboard sits between the POS and the dining floor.

FRONTEND PHASE 6 — INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
Objective

The Inventory module allows the Inventory Manager and Branch Manager to manage everything related to ingredients and suppliers.

Unlike POS where sales happen every minute, Inventory is focused on stock control.

The main objective is:

Always know what ingredients are available, what needs to be purchased, and what has already been received.
